About Me

I am Kaan Cebir, a junior mechanical engineering student at Izmir Katip Celebi University in Türkiye. My academic path is driven by a strong passion for scientific research and engineering innovation, particularly in the fields of aerospace technologies and turbomachinery. I strive to be more than just a manufacturing engineer—I aim to be a researcher and problem-solver who understands both science and application.

I have participated in engineering projects since my first year. I was part of the Formula Student R&D Team, where I focused on chassis design methodologies. I interned at Janoschka Aegean Cylinder Industry and contributed to the modernization of a copper stripping machine. I also collaborated with an M.Sc. student on CFD simulations of a hydrodynamic journal bearing and currently work with Assoc. Prof. Sercan Acarer on turbine blade cooling research.

My skills include ANSYS Fluent, MATLAB, Autodesk Inventor, and Python-based symbolic regression. I also received specialized training at TEI Aviation Engine School, where I gained a comprehensive understanding of gas turbine technologies and manufacturing processes.
